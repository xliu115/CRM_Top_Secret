## Problem Statement

Partners at a consulting firm manage dozens of high-value client relationships but have no proactive system to tell them who to contact, when, and why. The existing CRM has near-zero adoption because it demands manual data entry without delivering proportional value. Partners resort to memory, spreadsheets, PowerPoint, MS Planner, and personal notes — fragmenting relationship intelligence across disconnected tools. Relationships go cold silently. Opportunities are missed because no one surfaced the signal.

Activate already solves the "who to contact and why" problem through a consolidated nudge model (one card per contact, 9 signal types, AI-drafted emails). The gap is that it requires Partners to open a web app and manually refresh nudges — exactly the behavior pattern the existing CRM failed on.

## Demand Evidence

Strong demand claimed from two segments:
- **Senior Partners** — too many relationships to keep in their heads, rely on admin support
- **Newly promoted Partners** — building their book of business, need every signal and edge

The existing CRM's failure validates the problem: the tool exists but adoption is near-zero because it requires effort without proportional value. Partners are already doing manual versions of relationship tracking (Excel, PPT, calendar reminders, memory) — proving the problem is real and painful enough to work around.

## Status Quo

Partners currently use a fragmented stack:
1. **Optional CRM** (Salesforce/Dynamics) — very low adoption, clunky experience, painful data entry
2. **Excel spreadsheets** — personal contact lists, interaction logs
3. **PowerPoint** — client relationship summaries for internal presentations
4. **MS Planner / Notes** — personal task tracking for follow-ups
5. **Memory and gut feel** — the dominant "tool" for most Partners

The workaround cost: missed follow-ups, cold relationships, duplicated outreach (two Partners reaching out to the same person without coordination), zero visibility into relationship health at the firm level.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary:** Senior Partner managing 30-60+ relationships across 5-10 companies. Gets promoted based on client revenue, relationship depth, and new business development. Gets in trouble when key relationships go cold and competitors win the next engagement. Keeps them up at night: "Am I forgetting someone important?"

**Secondary:** Newly promoted Partner building their book. Needs every signal — job changes, news, event attendance, article engagement — to find conversation starters. Measured on relationship building velocity.

**Narrowest wedge:** A daily email/Teams notification that says "Here are your top 3 contacts to reach out to today, with draft emails ready to send." Zero login required. Zero effort. The Partner reads it over morning coffee and taps "send" on the drafts that look good.

## Constraints

1. Internal tool at a consulting firm — must meet enterprise security/compliance expectations
2. Data lives across multiple systems (CRM, Outlook, LinkedIn, internal databases) — integration complexity
3. Partners are extremely time-poor — any friction kills adoption
4. Must work alongside (not replace) the existing CRM
5. SQLite for dev, PostgreSQL for production — already architected
6. OpenAI GPT-4o-mini for AI features with template fallback — already built

## Premises

1. **Zero-effort value delivery is critical.** The existing CRM failed because it demanded work without proportional value. Activate must deliver value with ZERO Partner effort. If it requires manual input, adoption will be as low as the existing CRM.

2. **The nudge IS the product.** The #1 value proposition is "someone tells you exactly who to contact and why, with a draft email ready to send." Dashboard, contacts list, meetings page are supporting infrastructure. If you ship ONE thing to prove value, it's a daily push notification with top nudges + draft emails.

3. **Live data ingestion is the #1 gap.** The nudge engine is only as good as its data freshness. Without automatic data flow from calendars, email, LinkedIn, news feeds, and internal systems, nudges are stale and Partners lose trust.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Concierge Intelligence Platform (CHOSEN)
Double down on the consolidated nudge model + concierge tone. Add daily AI briefing push, one-click action without opening the app, relationship health scoring, cross-partner coordination, and analytics on nudge effectiveness. Makes Activate the Partner's AI chief of staff.
- Effort: XL (human: 3-4 weeks / CC: 3-4 hours)
- Risk: Medium
- Pros: Builds on strongest differentiation, proactive push solves adoption, analytics prove value
- Cons: Requires push notification infrastructure, more complex architecture

### Approach B: Data Pipeline + Polish (Minimal Viable)
Fix the #1 gap with live data connectors, then ship the 4 planned features from PRODUCT_REQUIREMENTS doc.
- Effort: L (human: 2 weeks / CC: 2 hours)
- Risk: Low
- Pros: Ships planned features, fixes data gap, lower risk
- Cons: Doesn't solve the adoption problem — still requires Partners to open the app

### Approach C: Ambient Intelligence (Creative/Lateral)
Remove the app as primary interface. Lives as Teams/Slack bot + email digest + Outlook add-in.
- Effort: XL (human: 4 weeks / CC: 4-5 hours)
- Risk: High
- Pros: Zero behavior change, meets Partners where they are
- Cons: High integration complexity, loses rich dashboard experience, harder to iterate

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A: Concierge Intelligence Platform** — because it builds on Activate's strongest differentiation (the consolidated one-card-per-contact nudge model with concierge tone) while adding the proactive push that makes adoption effortless. The daily AI briefing is the wedge that gets Partners hooked — they don't need to open the app to get value.

### Phase 1: Wedge (Ship First — ~1 hour CC time)
The minimum shippable slice that proves the core premise:
1. **Daily AI Briefing Email** — scheduled cron/API that runs `refreshNudgesForPartner`, picks top 3 nudges, generates AI summary + draft emails, sends via Resend. Partners read it over morning coffee. Default: top 3 nudges (configurable to 5 later).
2. **Auto-Refresh Nudge Engine** — scheduled daily refresh (cron job or API trigger) so nudges are never stale.
3. **Tavily News Pipeline** — already started in `news-ingestion-service.ts`. Wire it to run before nudge refresh so company news signals are fresh.

### Phase 2: Foundation (~1-2 hours CC time)
Infrastructure that supports the full vision:
4. **The 4 Planned Features:**
   - *Stale Contact Dashboard* — dedicated `/stale-contacts` page with urgency tiers, real-time staleness computation, importance filters. Uses same logic as nudge engine. (See PRODUCT_REQUIREMENTS_FOUR_FEATURES.md §1 for full spec.)
   - *Company-Level View* — `/companies/[id]` aggregating contacts, signals, interactions, meetings, and engagement per company. New company repository. (See §2.)
   - *Meeting Follow-Up Drafts* — `POST /api/meetings/[id]/follow-up-draft` for past meetings with thank-you + recap + action items. New LLM prompt. (See §3.)
   - *Configurable Nudge Rules* — per-Partner `NudgeRuleConfig` (already in schema). Settings UI at `/nudges/settings`. (See §4.)
5. **Relationship Health Scoring** — per-contact score (0-100) based on: interaction recency (40%), frequency (30%), signal density (20%), importance tier (10%). Per-company score = weighted average of contact scores. Stored as computed values on the dashboard, not persisted — recalculated on each dashboard load.

### Phase 3: Intelligence (~1-2 hours CC time)
The features that make this a platform:
6. **One-Click Actions from Email** — v1: deeplink in the briefing email that opens Activate with the draft pre-loaded and auto-focused. NOT server-side send — Partners must click "Send" in the app (respects firm email policies). Future: Outlook add-in for true one-click.
7. **Cross-Partner Coordination** — already partially built (`getPartnerRelationsForPerson` in nudge engine). Expand to: (a) show "Sam Rivera also knows this person" on nudge cards (done), (b) add a "Firm Relationships" tab on company pages showing all Partners with contacts there, (c) optional coordination alert in the daily briefing. Requires: company name normalization (already normalized via Company model) and Partner visibility rules (all Partners can see company-level coordination, not each other's interaction details).
8. **Nudge Analytics** — track: nudge viewed (email opened), nudge actioned (draft opened), email sent (from draft). New `NudgeAction` model with `nudgeId`, `action` (VIEWED/OPENED/SENT), `timestamp`. Dashboard chart: nudge effectiveness by type over time.

### Phase 4: Expansion (Future)
9. **Live Calendar Sync** — Microsoft Graph API for meeting auto-import. Blocked by: Graph API access + firm approval.
10. **LinkedIn Signal Feed** — blocked by: LinkedIn API access or browser extension approach. Deferred until data source is resolved.
11. **Teams/Slack Push** — blocked by: determining which platform Partners use. Default to email-only for v1.

## Edge Cases & Fallbacks

- **No CRM API:** Activate operates standalone with its own data. No sync needed for v1.
- **Two Partners "own" the same contact:** Already handled — contacts are scoped by `partnerId`. Cross-partner coordination shows visibility but doesn't share ownership.
- **Nudge engine errors / stale LLM/Tavily:** Template fallback already built for LLM. Tavily failure → nudges generated from existing signals only. Daily briefing skips news section if Tavily is down.
- **Unsubscribe / opt-out:** Add `briefingEnabled` boolean to Partner model. Briefing email includes unsubscribe link that sets this to false.
- **Timezone for daily briefing:** Use Partner's configured timezone (new field on Partner model, default UTC-5 EST). Send at 7am local time.
- **Contact name collision in cross-partner coordination:** Match by `(name, companyId)` pair, not name alone.

## Non-Functional Requirements

- **Data residency:** All data stored in firm-controlled PostgreSQL. No client PII sent to OpenAI — LLM prompts use contact names/titles but not emails, phone numbers, or account details.
- **Audit logging:** All email sends logged with timestamp, Partner, nudge ID, and action taken.
- **Retention:** Nudge history retained indefinitely for analytics. Briefing emails not stored after send.
- **Authentication:** NextAuth with credentials provider (existing). Production: integrate with firm's SSO (SAML/OIDC) — deferred to production deploy.

## Success Criteria

1. At least 5 Partners receive daily briefings for 2 weeks without complaining or unsubscribing
2. At least 1 Partner sends an email draft generated by Activate without significant edits
3. Nudge refresh runs automatically (not manually triggered) at least once per day
4. Partners can identify their "coldest" relationships in under 10 seconds
5. Cross-partner coordination prevents at least 1 duplicated outreach in the pilot period

## Distribution Plan

Internal web application deployed to the firm's infrastructure. Next.js app with PostgreSQL backend. Push notifications via email (Resend, already integrated). No public distribution — internal tool only. Existing deployment pipeline covers this.

## The Assignment

**Before building anything new:** Get 3 Partners to use the current version of Activate for 1 week. Don't demo it — sit behind them while they use it. Watch what they do. Watch what confuses them. Watch what they skip. The biggest risk isn't missing features — it's building the wrong features. The observation will tell you which of the 7 scope expansion items matters most.

Note: The pilot uses today's app (web-based, manual nudge refresh) to validate which pain points are real. The shipped product will be push-first / zero-login per the premises. The pilot is discovery, not the product.

If you can't get 3 Partners to try it, that's the real problem to solve first — not more features.
