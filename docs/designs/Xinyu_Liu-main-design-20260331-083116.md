## 1. One-Line Product Definition

A proactive relationship intelligence platform for consulting firm Partners that eliminates the manual effort of connecting dots across fragmented tools — surfacing who to contact, when, and why, with AI-drafted outreach ready to send and no relationship left to go cold.

## 2. Problem Statement (Why This Matters)

**The pain:** Partners manage hundred of high-value client relationships. Every missed follow-up is a relationship at risk. Every unprepared meeting is a missed opportunity. Every cold relationship is revenue walking to a competitor.

**Who experiences it:** Senior Partners managing large books of business (20+ clients, hundreds of contacts) and newly promoted Partners building their network from scratch. Both are measured on client revenue, relationship depth, and new business development.

**The cost today:** Relationship intelligence is scattered across isolated tools — Salesforce, Excel, PowerPoint, MS Planner, email threads, personal notes — with no connective tissue between them. Partners must manually cross-reference these systems to assemble a coherent picture of any client relationship: who last spoke to them, what was discussed, what's changed at their company, who else at the firm knows them. Every insight requires manual effort to surface and manual entry to record.

This fragmentation compounds across every relationship activity — meeting preparation, follow-up tracking, outreach planning, opportunity identification, cross-partner coordination. The firm invested in Salesforce, but adoption is near-zero because it demands manual data entry without delivering proportional value. Partners revert to spreadsheets and memory, which means relationship intelligence lives in individual heads, not in a system. When a Partner is busy, relationships go cold silently. When a Partner leaves, their institutional knowledge walks out the door.

**Industry context:** CRM adoption failure in professional services is widely cited at ~70% across industry analyses. The root cause is structural: these systems are built for management visibility, not individual productivity. They ask Partners to do work (data entry) to benefit someone else (leadership dashboards). The incentive is inverted.

**Works with sparse CRM data:** ClientIQ does not require a fully populated CRM. When Salesforce data is thin, the system enriches from calendar activity, email metadata, web intelligence (news, job changes), and meeting notes. The Contact 360 dossier always populates, scaling from "web-enriched profile" (minimal CRM) to "full relationship history" (rich CRM). This is by design: the tool must deliver value on day one, not after a data migration project.

## 3. Target Customer / Segment

**Primary user:** Senior Partner managing a portfolio of client relationships across multiple companies and industries. Gets promoted based on client revenue growth, relationship depth, and new logo wins. Gets in trouble when key relationships go cold and competitors win the next engagement.

**Secondary user:** Newly promoted Partner building their book of business. Needs every signal (job changes, company news, event attendance, article engagement) to find conversation starters and establish presence.

**Sponsor:** Practice leaders and senior leadership who need firm-wide visibility into relationship coverage, at-risk clients, and outreach coordination across Partners.

**Key personas:**

- **"The Plate Spinner"** — Senior Partner, 60+ active relationships, relies on memory and calendar reminders, fears forgetting someone important
- **"The Builder"** — Newly promoted Partner, 15-25 relationships, needs every edge to grow the book, lives on LinkedIn looking for conversation hooks
- **"The Practice Leader"** — Managing Partner, needs rollups on relationship health across the practice, currently asks each Partner individually

## 4. Design Principles

1. **Zero-effort by default**  
   If the Partner has to do work to get value, adoption dies. Every interaction must deliver more than it asks. The morning briefing email is the proof: open it, read it, tap send. Done. The system should do the heavy lifting in the background—auto-capturing signals, pre-filling suggestions, and minimizing manual logging wherever possible.

2. **Value-first interactions**  
   Partners don't want a table of interactions. They want a story: "Sarah hasn't heard from you in 3 weeks, she just got promoted, and she usually responds on Tuesday mornings. Here's a draft congratulations note." Context, not columns. The experience should lead with timely, actionable insight—not raw activity, records, or admin tasks.

3. **Personalized & cross-cutting**  
   The experience should work the way Partners work: across relationships, clients, CSTs, and practices—not inside one bounded account view. It should create value for the individual immediately, even before full team adoption.

4. **One contact, one truth**  
   Contact 360 is the canonical view. Every surface—dashboard, chat, email, mobile—draws from the same intelligence graph. No conflicting information across tools.

5. **In the flow of work**  
   The existing CRM failed because it waited for Partners to come to it. ClientIQ goes to the Partner. Push beats pull. Email first, notification second, app third. The app is one surface, not the center of gravity. The experience should show up in the channels where Partners already work—email, calendar, chat, mobile, and quick actions around meetings.

6. **Autonomy with managed visibility**  
   The system thinks, the Partner decides. ClientIQ recommends actions, drafts outreach, sequences follow-ups, and surfaces signals. The Partner retains full control: review, edit, send, dismiss. No black-box automation. All AI-generated content—emails, talking points, summaries—is presented as a draft for human review. When AI confidence is low or data is sparse, the system says so explicitly rather than fabricating detail. The same principle applies to collaboration: users should have control over what interactions, notes, and relationship details are visible to others.

## 6. Key Use Cases / Scenarios

### Use Case 1: The Morning Briefing

A Partner opens their email over morning coffee. ClientIQ's morning briefing tells them: their top contacts to reach out to today (with reasons), upcoming meetings with context, client news they should know about, and mini-360 snapshots of key contacts. They tap "send" on a pre-drafted email to a contact they haven't spoken to in 3 weeks. No cross-referencing tools. No CRM login required.

### Use Case 2: Pre-Meeting Intelligence

A Partner has a client meeting in 30 minutes. They open ClientIQ's Contact 360 on their phone: a 7-section dossier covering the contact's background, relationship history, firm-wide connections, recent news, open action items, and 3 AI-generated talking points. They walk in prepared. The contact notices.

### Use Case 3: The "Ask Anything" Conversational Query

A Partner remembers a contact but can't recall the details. They open the chat and type "What's the latest with Sarah Chen?" ClientIQ returns a Quick 360: an insight summary, talking points, and quick actions (full Contact 360, Company 360, draft email, share dossier). The Partner taps "Draft Email" and a context-aware email is generated inline. No page navigation.

### Use Case 4: Automated Relationship Cadence

A Partner initiated outreach to a prospect 5 days ago with no response. ClientIQ's cadence engine automatically generates a follow-up with a different angle. If the contact responds (detected via inbound email scan or manual confirmation), the sequence pauses. If no response after 3 touches, it escalates with a suggested warm introduction through a mutual connection. The Partner never has to remember to follow up.

## 7. Product Overview (What It Does)

**Delivery model:** Primary value is push-based (morning briefing email, nudge notifications) and requires no app login. The web dashboard, mobile interface, and conversational chat are optional power surfaces for Partners who want to go deeper. This resolves the adoption problem: the CRM failed because it required login; ClientIQ delivers value to the inbox.

**Terminology:**

- **360** = AI-generated intelligence dossier (Contact 360 = person, Company 360 = organization)
- **Mini-360** = 3-section excerpt in morning briefing emails
- **Quick 360** = Summarized insight + talking points in chat
- **Nudge** = Actionable recommendation to contact someone, with reason and draft outreach
- **Cadence** = Multi-step automated follow-up sequence

**What's built vs. what's next:**


| Status              | Capability                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Built               | Morning Briefing Email, Nudge Engine (9 signal types), Contact 360, Company 360, Quick 360, Ask Anything Chat, Cadence Engine, Context-Aware Email Drafting, Desktop Dashboard, Mobile Conversational Interface |
| Pilot scope (day 1) | Morning Briefing Email, Nudge Engine, Contact 360, Email Drafting. Pending: Salesforce read API + email domain approval                                                                                         |
| Roadmap             | Relationship Health Dashboard, Firm-Wide Relationship Graph, Nudge Effectiveness Analytics, Connection Strength Scoring, Tone-of-Voice Personalization                                                          |


### Pillar 1: Proactive Intelligence Delivery

- **Morning Briefing Email** — AI-generated narrative weaving nudges, meetings, news, and relationship context into a 2-minute daily read with mini-360 snippets
- **Nudge Engine** — 9 signal types (stale contact, missed meeting, news trigger, job change, sentiment shift, engagement drop, opportunity signal, relationship decay, custom) prioritized and delivered as actionable cards
- **Push-First Architecture** — Value delivered via email/notification before the Partner ever opens the app

### Pillar 2: Deep Relationship Intelligence

- **Contact 360** — 7-section AI dossier combining CRM data with live web intelligence (profile, relationship history, communication timeline, firm connections, news/signals, action items, talking points)
- **Company 360** — 5-section company intelligence (overview, coverage map, relationship health matrix, signals/news, strategic recommendations)
- **Quick 360** — Summarized insight + talking points delivered in conversational chat with contextual quick actions

### Pillar 3: Conversational AI Interface

- **Ask Anything Chat** — Natural language queries across all CRM data and web intelligence with RAG-powered retrieval
- **Contextual Quick Actions** — Dynamic action buttons in every AI response (Contact 360, Company 360, Draft Email, Share) with thread-aware memory that avoids repeating actions
- **Intent Detection** — Expanded to handle structured queries (relationship recap, meeting prep, contact search, nudge config)

### Pillar 4: Automated Outreach

- **Cadence Engine** — Multi-step relationship workflows: initial outreach → follow-up on no response → angle adjustment → escalation or archive. State machine with configurable timing.
- **Context-Aware Email Drafting** — AI-generated emails pre-loaded with relationship context, recent interactions, and relevant news
- **One-Click Send** — Draft emails ready to send from morning briefing, nudge cards, or chat. Default: generates draft for Partner review and manual send. Server-side send available pending firm email policy approval.

### Pillar 5: Multi-Surface Access

- **Desktop Dashboard** — Full-featured web application with sidebar navigation, settings, and rich data views
- **Mobile Conversational Interface** — Single-surface chat-first design where the briefing IS the homepage, LLM IS the navigation. 60-second mobile experience.
- **Email** — Morning briefings, mini-360 snippets, and shareable dossiers delivered directly to inbox

**Competitive landscape (March 2026):**

**Structural advantages:**

1. **Internal data access** — Reads Salesforce, calendars, email metadata, meeting notes, and internal databases that external vendors would need months of integration work to access. Access scope subject to firm IT/legal policy; pilot requires read-only Salesforce API and calendar permissions.
2. **Weekly iteration** — Ships improvements every week, not every quarter. No vendor roadmap dependencies.
3. **Zero procurement** — No 6-12 month buying cycle. Deploy to one Partner next week.
4. **Push-first architecture** — Competitors build apps Partners have to open. ClientIQ sends intelligence to where Partners already are.
5. **Full-stack intelligence + action** — Most competitors do intelligence OR outreach. ClientIQ does both: surfaces the signal AND drafts the response AND sequences the follow-up.

## 9. Business Impact / Metrics

### Efficiency Gains

- **Eliminated tool fragmentation:** Partners currently cross-reference 5+ disconnected systems to assemble relationship context. ClientIQ consolidates this into a single intelligence layer, removing the manual dot-connecting entirely.
- **Relationship monitoring at scale:** From zero systematic coverage to every contact in the book monitored daily. The system surfaces at-risk relationships before the Partner notices.
- **Follow-up completion:** From ad-hoc tracking (calendar reminders, sticky notes, memory) to automated cadence sequences that persist until resolved.

### Revenue Impact

- **At-risk relationship recovery:** Each saved client relationship is worth $500K-$5M+ in annual revenue to a consulting firm. Preventing even 1-2 relationship lapses per Partner per year pays for the platform many times over.
- **Faster new business development:** New Partners with systematic intelligence outperform those relying on memory. Earlier and more relevant outreach = shorter sales cycles.
- **Cross-sell identification:** Company 360's coverage map reveals which contacts are under-served, where competing firms have deeper relationships, and where expansion opportunities exist.

### Strategic Value

- **Firm-wide relationship visibility** — Practice leaders get rollups on coverage, at-risk accounts, and coordination for the first time. No more asking each Partner individually.
- **Knowledge retention** — When a Partner leaves, their relationship intelligence stays in ClientIQ. Currently, it walks out the door.
- **Competitive moat** — Internal tool can't be replicated by competitors. Built on proprietary data, firm-specific workflows, and institutional knowledge that no SaaS vendor can access.

## Cross-Model Perspective

An independent Claude subagent reviewed this session cold:

- **Steelman:** A push-first "relationship copilot" that uses existing Salesforce + email/calendar metadata to tell Partners who to touch before it's awkward, what to say, and one concrete next step, delivered in email/Teams.
- **Key insight:** The push-first premise collapses the product from "CRM replacement" into a delivery design. Success is measured by inbox engagement and executed follow-ups, not UI depth.
- **Challenged premise:** Argued that portfolio visibility matters alongside push nudges. Partners may need weekly rollups ("show me my at-risk accounts") as a complementary artifact. This was accepted and incorporated.
- **48-hour prototype:** Nightly job pulling contacts + last activity + upcoming meetings, generating 3 sections (tomorrow's meetings, at-risk contacts, one-click actions), sending one morning email per partner. Skip everything else.

## Premises

1. Partners won't adopt another CRM that requires regular login. Primary value must be push-based (email, Teams). The web dashboard and mobile app are optional power surfaces for deeper workflows, not the adoption gate.
2. The firm's internal Salesforce has enough data (contacts, companies, some activity) to anchor enrichment, even with low adoption. ClientIQ enriches sparse CRM data from calendars, email metadata, and web intelligence.
3. Primary value is (a) "save meeting prep time" and (b) "don't let relationships go cold" for the individual Partner, AND (c) portfolio visibility and coverage analytics for the sponsor/practice leader. Both are first-class value props, delivered through different interfaces.
4. Building internally beats buying because: internal data access, weekly iteration, no 6-12 month procurement cycle.
5. The MVP user is ONE Partner who uses this daily. Firm-wide rollout is later.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "Concierge-First" — Minimal Viable Positioning

Position ClientIQ as a push-first relationship concierge. Lead with the morning email and meeting prep. The exec doc focuses on one user, one workflow, one metric (time saved per meeting).

- Effort: S (human: ~2 hrs / CC: ~15 min)
- Risk: Low
- Pros: Sharp narrative, easy sponsor buy-in
- Cons: Undersells what's already built

### Approach B: "Intelligence Platform" — Full Vision (CHOSEN)

Position ClientIQ as a comprehensive relationship intelligence platform. Cover all shipped capabilities and articulate how they compose into an end-to-end system. Include competitive positioning.

- Effort: M (human: ~1 day / CC: ~30 min)
- Risk: Medium
- Pros: Captures everything built, strong differentiation story
- Cons: Higher bar for sponsor comprehension

### Approach C: "Wedge + Roadmap" — Strategic Narrative

Lead with the narrow wedge (morning briefing) as the "today" story, frame the rest as roadmap. Tells a story from start to expansion.

- Effort: M (human: ~4 hrs / CC: ~25 min)
- Risk: Low
- Pros: Incremental buy-in path
- Cons: Could feel like bait-and-switch

## Recommended Approach

Approach B — Intelligence Platform. The product is built. The capabilities are real. The exec doc should reflect the full vision and position ClientIQ as a strategic asset, not a side project. The sponsor needs to see ambition that matches the investment being requested.

## Open Questions

1. **Data access permissions:** What level of Salesforce API access can be provisioned for ClientIQ? Read-only sufficient for MVP, but real-time sync requires event subscriptions.
2. **Email sending authorization:** Can ClientIQ send emails on behalf of Partners, or must it generate drafts they send manually? Compliance implications.
3. **Pilot Partner selection:** Who is the first Partner to use this daily? Ideal: a Senior Partner who is vocal about the problem, has 30+ active relationships, and would champion the tool internally.
4. **Calendar integration:** Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace? Determines the meeting data pipeline.
5. **Firm security review:** Will the platform need to pass an internal security assessment before pilot? Timeline implications.
6. **Reply detection scope:** Cadence engine detects replies via inbound email scan. Accuracy depends on mail system access (Exchange API, threading fidelity). Pilot fallback: Partners manually confirm replies. Full automation requires email integration beyond Salesforce.
